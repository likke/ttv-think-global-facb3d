01 /The opportunity

Thousands of students need real AI skills. The credible programs are overseas-priced and not built for Filipino classrooms.

  • The need is national — across regions, levels, and thousands of learners.
  • Imported programs are expensive, generic, and hard to scale with quality.
  • The win is a locally-relevant, school-ready program that's credible enough to certify and repeatable enough to run everywhere.
02 /What students walk away with

Not a lecture they forget — a capability they keep. By the end of the program every learner can:

  • Explain AI in plain language and judge where it fails
  • Use AI tools responsibly — privacy, bias, verification, ethics
  • Build something real — a project, workflow, or presentation, made with AI
  • Present it with confidence, and leave with a certificate that means something
03 /The shape

A three-day journey — roughly 70% hands-on, differentiated by student level, and built to be delivered by a certified facilitator network so it scales without losing quality.

Day 1

Understand AI

Literacy, responsible use, and the judgment to tell good output from bad.

Day 2

Build With AI

Turn a real problem into a working, AI-assisted output.

Day 3

Present & Reflect

Finish, present, assess — and certify.

The detailed curriculum — the day-by-day design, activities, and assessments — is the build itself, so it stays under wraps until we're formally working together. This is the shape; it's all you need to decide.

05 /Two ways to build this together

There's more than one way to structure this — and the right one depends on how big Think Global wants to go. We'll walk the numbers through live.

Track A · Engagement

I build it, you run it

I design and certify the program; your team delivers it at scale under license. Clean and fully de-risked.

Track B · Partnership

We build it together

I come in as Program Architect and we share the upside as it scales nationally — a program with both our names on it.

Either way: I design and own the curriculum and certify the facilitators; you own the school rollout. That split is what lets this scale without the quality slipping. The commercial model for each path is for our conversation.
